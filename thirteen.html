Pulihora (Tamarind Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 tablespoons peanuts
  • 4-5 dry red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Few curry leaves

Instructions:

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water for 15 minutes. Extract the juice and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add chana dal, urad dal, peanuts, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the dals turn golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric powder and mix well.
  4. Add the tamarind juice, salt, and cook until the mixture thickens.
  5. Add cooked rice and mix gently until well combined.
  6. Serve pulihora hot or at room temperature.

Gongura Pachadi (Sorrel Leaves Chutney)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh sorrel leaves (gongura)
  • 4-5 green chilies
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Clean and wash sorrel leaves. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  3. Add the sorrel leaves, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes until the leaves wilt and soften.
  4. Allow the mixture to cool. Then, grind it to a coarse paste.
  5. Gongura pachadi is ready to be served with rice or roti.

Pesarattu (Moong Dal Dosa)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole green moong dal (split green gram)
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Soak moong dal in water for 4-5 hours. Drain the water and rinse the dal.
  2. In a blender, grind the dal along with green chilies, ginger, asafoetida, and salt. Add water as needed to make a smooth batter.
  3. Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it in a circular motion to form a dosa.
  4. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crisp.
  5. Serve pesarattu hot with chutney or sambar.

Pulao (Mixed Vegetable Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, etc.), chopped
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. Wash rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pressure cooker or a deep pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add chopped vegetables, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Add soaked and drained rice. Stir gently to combine with the vegetables.
  6. Add water, cover the pressure cooker, and cook for 2-3 whistles or until the rice is cooked.
  7. Allow the pressure to release naturally. Fluff the pulao with a fork before serving.

Ariselu (Sweet Rice Flour Fritters)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup jaggery, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, grated jaggery, and cardamom powder.
  2. Add water gradually and knead the mixture to form a smooth dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small portions and shape each portion into a round disc or a flat fritter.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai. Fry the ariselu on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  5. Drain the fritters on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  6. Ariselu can be stored in an airtight container and enjoyed as a sweet treat.
