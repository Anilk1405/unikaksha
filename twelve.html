Charminar

Location: Hyderabad

Charminar is a historical monument and iconic symbol of Hyderabad. Built in 1591, it is known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings. The Charminar is located in the heart of the old city and is surrounded by bustling markets.

Tirupati Temple

Location: Tirupati

Tirupati Temple, also known as Sri Venkateswara Temple, is one of the most famous and sacred Hindu temples in India. Located on the Tirumala hills, it attracts millions of devotees every year. The temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara and showcases magnificent Dravidian architecture.

Borra Caves

Location: Visakhapatnam

Borra Caves are natural limestone formations situated in the Ananthagiri Hills of the Eastern Ghats. These caves, discovered in 1807, are known for their stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations. The cave complex offers a unique experience for nature enthusiasts and spelunkers.

Kondapalli Fort

Location: Vijayawada

Kondapalli Fort is a historic hill fortress located in Kondapalli village near Vijayawada. Built during the 14th century, the fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It is known for its intricate architecture, moat, and various structures within the fort complex.

Belum Caves

Location: Kurnool

Belum Caves are the second largest natural caves in India, situated in the Belum Village of Kurnool district. The cave system is renowned for its unique formations, including stalactites, stalagmites, and underground chambers. It is a popular destination for spelunking and exploration.
